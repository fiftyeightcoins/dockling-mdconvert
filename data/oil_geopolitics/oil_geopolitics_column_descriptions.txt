FILE 1: oil_prices_daily.csv  (9,214 rows — daily, Jan 1990 – Apr 2025)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

date                    Trading date (YYYY-MM-DD), weekdays only

── Crude Oil Benchmarks ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
brent_usd               Brent crude spot price (USD per barrel)
                        North Sea blend; global pricing benchmark for ~2/3 of world oil
                        Historical range: $11.55 (Dec 1998) → $147 (Jul 2008)
                        Calibrated to: EIA European Brent Spot Price

wti_usd                 West Texas Intermediate spot price (USD per barrel)
                        US domestic benchmark; priced at Cushing, Oklahoma hub
                        Typically trades at $1–4 discount to Brent (quality premium)
                        Exception: 2011–2013 WTI traded at $15–20 DISCOUNT due
                        to US shale glut and lack of export infrastructure

dubai_usd               Dubai/Oman crude spot price (USD per barrel)
                        Sour crude benchmark for Middle East → Asia flows
                        Relevant for: pricing Iran, Saudi, UAE exports to China/India
                        Typically $0.50–$3.00 below Brent (sour crude discount)

brent_wti_spread        Brent minus WTI (USD per barrel)
                        Positive = Brent premium (normal)
                        Negative = WTI premium (rare; US shale glut 2011–2013)
                        Useful as geopolitical stress indicator:
                        Brent spikes more than WTI during Middle East events

── Volatility & Risk ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
brent_daily_return_pct  Daily log return of Brent price (%)
                        Computed as: ln(Pt / Pt-1) × 100
                        Normal range: ±2%; crisis days: ±10–15%

brent_30d_vol           30-day rolling standard deviation of daily returns
                        Annualized volatility proxy (multiply by √252 for annual)
                        Low vol regime: ~15%; crisis regime: ~60–80%

hormuz_risk_premium_pct Estimated Strait of Hormuz risk premium (% of Brent price)
                        0–2%: Normal; 2–5%: Elevated tension; 5–8%: High risk
                        Calibrated to documented premium during:
                          2012 Iran closure threat: ~4–6%
                          2019 tanker attacks: ~3–5%
                          2024–2025 Houthi/Iran escalation: ~4–8%

── Event Flags ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
is_geopolitical_event   Binary: 1 if a major geopolitical event occurred on this date
                        Links to geopolitical_events.csv for event details
                        68 event dates across 35 years

event_type              Type of event (null if no event):
                          military     — Armed conflict, strike, invasion
                          sanctions    — New sanctions imposed or lifted
                          diplomatic   — Agreement, talks, deal
                          market       — OPEC decision, production change
                          political    — Election, regime change, policy shift
                          economic     — Financial crisis, recession
                          terror       — Terrorist attack

event_description       Brief description of the event (null if no event)

── Supply Indicators ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
opec_production_mbd     OPEC total crude oil production (million barrels per day)
                        Historical range: ~24–35 mbd
                        Calibrated to: IEA/EIA OPEC production estimates

us_rig_count            US active oil drilling rig count (Baker Hughes style)
                        Leading indicator for US production 3–6 months ahead
                        Peak: ~1,900 (2014) | COVID trough: ~200 (2020)
                        Recovery: ~700 (2022–2024)

FILE 2: geopolitical_events.csv  (68 rows — key events 1990–2025)

date                    Event date (YYYY-MM-DD)
event_name              Full event name/description
event_type              military / sanctions / diplomatic / market / political / economic / terror
region                  Middle East / Global
iran_involved           Binary: 1 if Iran is a direct participant in the event
price_shock_pct         Estimated immediate Brent price shock (%, positive = up)

                        NOT a precise causal estimate; use as approximate signal
shock_direction         up / down / neutral
duration_days           Estimated market impact duration (days)
severity                extreme / high / medium / low
strait_of_hormuz_risk   Binary: 1 if event threatens Strait of Hormuz shipping
sanctions_related       Binary: 1 if event is sanctions-driven
military_action         Binary: 1 if event involves military force

FILE 3: iran_oil_exports.csv  (360 rows — annual by destination, 1990–2025)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

year                    Calendar year (1990–2025)
destination             Export destination country/region:
                          China, India, Japan, South Korea, Italy, Turkey,
                          Greece, Spain, Syria, Other
exports_mbd             Iran crude oil exports to this destination (million barrels/day)
total_iran_exports_mbd  Iran total crude exports across all destinations (mbd)
                        Historical range:
                          Peak: ~2.6 mbd (pre-sanctions, 2005–2011)
                          Trough: ~0.7 mbd (maximum pressure, 2019–2021)
share_pct               This destination's share of total Iran exports (%)
sanctions_status        Sanctions regime in effect:
                          none     — Minimal US sanctions (pre-2012)
                          moderate — Partial restrictions
                          heavy    — Full EU+US+UN embargo (2012–2015, 2019–2022)
is_sanctions_year       Binary: 1 if heavy sanctions were in effect this year
estimated_revenue_bn_usd Approximate annual oil revenue (billion USD)
                        Computed as: exports × 365 × avg_price × 0.8 (discount)

FILE 4: sanctions_timeline.csv  (25 rows — sanction events 1979–2025)

financial_sector_affected Binary: 1 if sanction targets banking/financial sector
is_relief               Binary: 1 if this is a sanctions relief (not tightening)
cumulative_pressure_score Running sum of GDP impact — shows cumulative sanctions burden

FILE 5: risk_indicators.csv  (424 rows — monthly, Jan 1990 – Apr 2025)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

date                    First day of month (YYYY-MM-DD)
year / month            Calendar year and month

── Geopolitical Risk ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
geopolitical_risk_index Composite geopolitical risk index (GPR)
                        Calibrated to: Caldara & Iacoviello (2022) GPR Index
                        Baseline ~100; spikes during crises:
                          9/11 (2001):     ~400
                          Iraq War (2003): ~320
                          Ukraine (2022):  ~380
                          Iran escalation (2025): ~260

iran_specific_risk_bp   Iran-specific geopolitical risk premium (basis points)
                        Added to Brent price as estimated Iran risk component
                        Low: 50–150 bps (normal periods)
                        High: 200–500 bps (heavy sanctions / conflict)
                        1 basis point = 0.01% of price

── Financial Stress ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
vix_index               CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) — implied S&P 500 volatility
                        "Fear gauge" for global risk appetite
                        Low: <15 (calm); High: >30 (stress); Crisis: >50
                        Peak: ~80 (March 2020 COVID)

usd_index_dxy           US Dollar Index (DXY) — USD vs basket of 6 currencies
                        Strong USD (high DXY) → lower oil prices in USD terms
                        Inverse correlation with oil well documented

gold_usd_oz             Gold spot price (USD per troy oz)
                        Safe-haven asset; rises with geopolitical risk
                        Range: $380 (1990) → $2,750 (2024)

── Supply/Demand Balance ────────────────────────────────────────────────────
opec_spare_capacity_mbd OPEC spare production capacity (million barrels/day)
                        Key supply buffer: low spare capacity → high prices
                        Normal: 2–4 mbd; Crisis: <1 mbd; Glut: >5 mbd

── Derived Risk Metrics ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
straits_closure_prob_pct Estimated probability of Strait of Hormuz disruption (%)
                         0–5%: Normal; 5–15%: Elevated; 15–30%: High tension
                         ~20% of world oil passes through Strait of Hormuz

brent_risk_premium_usd  Estimated geopolitical risk premium embedded in Brent price (USD/bbl)
                         = portion of price attributable to geopolitical factors
                         vs. pure supply/demand fundamentals
                         Normal: $2–5; Crisis: $10–25

energy_security_index   Composite energy security score (0–100)
                         Higher = more secure; lower = more vulnerable
                         Incorporates: GPR, VIX, spare capacity, USD strength
                         Useful as single summary variable for geopolitical risk

KEY CALIBRATION SOURCES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

EIA (US Energy Information Administration)
  → Brent/WTI/Dubai daily spot prices: eia.gov/petroleum/data
  → OPEC production data, US rig count history

IEA (International Energy Agency)
  → Oil Market Reports — OPEC spare capacity estimates
  → Iran export data by destination country

Caldara & Iacoviello (2022)
  "Measuring Geopolitical Risk", American Economic Review
  → GPR Index methodology and historical series
  → Available: matteoiacoviello.com/gpr.htm

OFAC (US Treasury Office of Foreign Assets Control)
  → Iran sanctions timeline: dates, categories, scope

IMF World Economic Outlook
  → Iran GDP impact estimates for sanctions periods

BP Statistical Review of World Energy 2023
  → Historical oil price series, production by country
  → Strait of Hormuz volume estimates (~20% of global oil)

IMPORTANT NOTES FOR ANALYSIS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

LOG SCALE: Brent price spans $11–$147 over 35 years.
  For time series visualization, consider log scale or returns.
  brent_daily_return_pct is already in return space.

EVENT STUDY: price_shock_pct in geopolitical_events.csv is
  approximate. For rigorous event study, compute:
    CAR(t) = Σ [R(t+i) - E[R(t+i)]] for i in [-5, +30]
  using brent_daily_return_pct from oil_prices_daily.csv.

NEGATIVE PRICES: WTI went negative on April 20, 2020 (storage crisis).
  This is in the daily data. Be careful with log transforms —
  use brent_usd (never went negative) for log-return calculations.
